  • Version Timeline: HTTP evolved from 0.9 (GET-only) to 1.0/1.1 (headers, persistent connections, caching) to 2 (binary framing, multiplexing) and 3 (QUIC over UDP) to cut latency and overhead.
  • Methods & Status Codes: Methods like GET (read), POST (submit), and HEAD (metadata) pair with status codes such as 200 OK, 301/302 redirects, 404 Not Found, and 500 Server Error to describe outcomes.
  • HTTP/2 Performance: HTTP/2 speeds pages by multiplexing many streams over one TCP connection, compressing headers with HPACK, and prioritizing critical resources.
  • HTTP/3 & QUIC: HTTP/3 runs over QUIC/UDP to avoid transport head-of-line blocking, enable faster encrypted handshakes, and perform better on mobile or lossy networks.
